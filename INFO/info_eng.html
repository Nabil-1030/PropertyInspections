Information & Property Inspections

Everything you need to know about property inspections, our working method, property types and legal certainty.

Who are we?

Property Inspections is an independent real estate expert specialised in entry and exit property inspections. We work for both landlords and tenants and guarantee an objective, neutral and legally correct approach.

Our mission is clear: preventing disputes through a clear, transparent and professional report accepted by all parties. Thanks to our experience in the Belgian rental market, we know exactly what to focus on.

What is a property inspection?

A property inspection is a detailed written report describing the condition of a property at a specific moment, usually at the start (entry) or the end (exit) of a rental agreement.

  • Condition of walls, ceilings and floors
  • Windows, doors and joinery
  • Kitchen, sanitary installations and bathroom
  • Visible technical installations
  • Any damage or wear and tear

The inspection is based on objective findings and supported by photographs. It is an essential legal document for the release of the rental guarantee.

Why is a property inspection so important?

Without a valid inspection report, it is very difficult to correctly attribute damage or repairs at the end of a tenancy.

  • Protection for landlords: proof that damage was not present at entry
  • Protection for tenants: no liability for pre-existing defects
  • Prevention of disputes and legal proceedings
  • Clarity when releasing the rental deposit

A correct property inspection avoids conflicts and saves time, money and stress for all parties.

When is a property inspection carried out?

Entry inspection

Conducted at the start of the rental agreement, before or shortly after the handover of the keys. This report describes the initial condition of the property.

Exit inspection

Conducted at the end of the rental agreement. The current condition of the property is compared with the entry inspection to determine any damage.

Legal framework

The inspection is carried out in accordance with the Belgian Civil Code and regional rental legislation.

  • Must be conducted on a contradictory basis (all parties invited)
  • Attached to the rental agreement
  • Has evidential value in case of disputes

The report becomes legally binding after payment and the expiry of the legal comment period.
